Forester Robots

Could robots be the future of forests?

The Multiscope Forester Planter by Milrem Robotics has been designed to easily plant trees, carrying up to 380 saplings, and can plant a hectare of land in 5-6.5 hours depending on the type of tree and the terrain it is trying to plant on. They can also be used to prepare ground for planting and plough snow, potentially allowing for automated planting of crops.

The robots use Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging) which allows them to generate a 3D image of the surroundings using laser pulses. Combined with a GPS system it can precisely navigate terrain as well as store the exact location of the sapling as it is planted.

This information can then be used by the Multiscope Forester Brushcutter to cut back any potentially competing undergrowth to ensure the saplings can get the water and nutrients they need to grow. As it knows the location where the sapling is planted it won't damage the sapling and just cut around it.
